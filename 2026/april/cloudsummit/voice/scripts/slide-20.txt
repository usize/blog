Let's also think about policy scoping.

When we're applying ingress oriented policies, our policies tend to be scoped to the Gateway itself – e.g., a global TLS policy – or to individual routes.

When we're applying credential injection policies, this can be fraught.

Applying at the Gateway increases our odds of leaking credentials in plaintext logs when the wrong endpoint gets credentials.

Attaching per route increases operational overhead and makes it more likely we'll fail to rotate credentials.

Because of this, some egress gateways and some AI Gateways offer a policy scoped per backend.
