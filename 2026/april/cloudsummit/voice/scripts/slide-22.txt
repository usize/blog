Putting it all together, we can see a very "complete" set of operations that one might use in a very sophisticated policy chain.

In a real scenario, some of these might be able to run in parallel, but, for now, let's notice how we've tried to strike a balance between policies that let us bail out early and cheaply, versus policies that are more expensive but supply information that's necessary to proceed further through the pipeline.

It's also important to note that when a 'fallback' happens to another provider we may need to jump back into the pipeline or move over to a 'fallback policy chain'. That way we can re-inject credentials for example.
